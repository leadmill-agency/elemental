# Element Doors & Garages — Website Brief

## Project Goal
Build a **real homepage** for Element Doors & Garages, plus a **child landing page** that can be used for Meta ads.

This is **not** a generic redesign.

The goal is to create a site that feels:
- modern
- local
- trustworthy
- calm
- slightly premium
- real

It should avoid looking like:
- AI slop
- a generic SaaS site
- a contractor template
- a flashy agency mockup
- a national chain landing page

The homepage should explain the business clearly and route visitors into the main service paths.
The landing page should be narrower and more conversion-focused.

---

## Business Context
Current site: Element Doors & Garages

The current site has useful ingredients:
- Houston positioning
- trusted since 2003
- call and text behavior
- same-day service messaging
- residential and commercial support
- strong garage door imagery

But the current homepage is too broad and too noisy for strong conversion.
It mixes too many intents at once:
- commercial
- residential
- repairs
- installs
- emergency service
- spring repairs
- gallery
- FAQs
- blog

That makes it feel more like a catch-all website than a disciplined homepage.

---

## Strategy
### Correct site architecture
- **Homepage** = broad but disciplined
- **Landing page** = narrow and conversion-heavy

Do **not** try to make the homepage behave like a Meta landing page.
Do **not** try to make the landing page behave like a broad homepage.

The homepage should answer these questions in order:
1. What does Element do?
2. Where do they do it?
3. Why should I trust them?
4. What are the main paths I can take?
5. What should I do next?

- fake 3D illustrations

### What we want instead
Use:

- strong typography
- restrained spacing
- clean hierarchy
- simple section structure
- grounded trust signals
- real local proof
- purposeful design decisions

Style statement:
**Modern local service brand. Editorial restraint. Real photos. Strong typography. Clean spacing. No gimmicks.**

Short version:
**Not startup. Not contractor cliché. Not AI slop. Clean Houston premium.**

## Homepage Structure
### 1. Header
Keep the navigation tight.

Recommended nav:
- Services
- Service Areas
- About
- Reviews
- Contact

Primary CTA:
- Call Now
or
- Get a Quote

Do not keep a giant top nav with blog, gallery, FAQs, spring repairs, other services, etc.

### 2. Hero
Headline:
**Garage Door Repair & Installation in Houston**

Subhead:
Same-day service available for repairs, replacements, and new garage doors. Serving Houston homeowners and businesses since 2003.

Primary CTAs:
- Call Now
- Get a Free Quote

Trust row beneath:
- Serving Houston since 2003
- Same-day service available
- Residential + commercial
- Call or text today

### 3. Social Proof / Trust Strip
Place directly below the hero.

Can include:
- review rating if real
- number of reviews if real
- local trust statements
- fast response
- Houston-based service

### 4. Main Service Paths
Three cards only:

**Repair**
Broken spring, off-track door, opener issues, same-day help.

**Installation**
Upgrade your home with a new garage door built for style and performance.

**Commercial**
Reliable commercial garage door service for Houston businesses.

This is the right amount of choice.
Not 8 to 12 cards.

### 5. Why Choose Element
Use 4 to 6 proof points max.
Examples:
- Serving Houston since 2003
- Residential and commercial expertise
- Same-day service available
- Real local team
- Quality parts and installation
- Clear communication from quote to completion

Only use claims that are real.
Do not invent trust cues.

### 6. Featured Work / Visual Proof
Kill the slider.

Use a clean static grid instead:
- one modern home installation
- one premium-looking garage door
- one commercial install

Optional CTA:
- View More Projects

### 7. Process Section
Headline:
**How It Works**

Three steps:
1. Call or request a quote
2. We assess the job and recommend the right solution
3. We repair, replace, or install with clear next steps

### 8. Reviews / Testimonials
Use 2 to 4 strong testimonials.
No giant review wall.
No endless carousel.

### 9. Service Area Block
Short local section listing major Houston-area coverage.
Examples:
- Houston
- Katy
- Cypress
- Sugar Land
- Spring
- The Woodlands
- Pearland

### 10. Final CTA
Headline:
**Need Garage Door Help Fast?**

Copy:
Call or text Element for a fast quote on repair, replacement, or installation.

Buttons:
- Call Now
- Text Us

### 11. Footer
Minimal footer only.

Can include:
- phone
- address
- service area
- hours
- social if needed

Avoid giant sitemaps.

## Landing Page Strategy
One child page should function as the Meta landing page.

Recommended page:
**Garage Door Repair Houston**

URL idea:
`/garage-door-repair-houston`

### Job of the landing page
The landing page should:
- match ad intent more tightly
- reduce distractions
- focus on one core offer
- increase calls and quote requests

### Primary offer
**Garage Door Repair in Houston with same-day service available**

### Landing page audience
Houston homeowners with urgent or semi-urgent issues:
- broken spring
- opener issue
- off-track door
- garage door won’t open
- noisy or jerky garage door

### Landing page structure
1. Hero with narrower repair-focused headline
2. Trust/review strip
3. Common problems we fix
4. Why choose Element
5. Simple 3-step process
6. Testimonials + real photo section
7. Strong CTA block
8. Short FAQ
9. Minimal footer

### Landing page difference from homepage
Homepage:
- broader
- routes across service paths
- explains company clearly

Landing page:
- narrower
- more direct
- fewer nav links
- more CTA repetition
- more problem-specific
- built for paid traffic
